# 🧠 Prompts for Ideation

> Your best ideas start with a great question. Copy-paste these prompts into your favorite AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.) and watch the magic happen. Swap out the bracketed `[placeholders]` with your own details!

---

## Brainstorming App Ideas

### 1. Generate ideas based on your skills and interests

```
I'm a [your profession/hobby, e.g., "freelance photographer"] and I notice that
[describe a frustration or inefficiency you experience]. Can you brainstorm 10
simple web app ideas that could solve this problem or make this easier? For each
idea, give me a one-sentence description and who would use it.
```

### 2. Spot opportunities in your daily life

```
Here's a list of things I do every week that feel annoying, repetitive, or
time-consuming:

- [task 1]
- [task 2]
- [task 3]

For each one, suggest a simple app or tool that could make it easier. Keep the
ideas small enough that one person could build them with AI coding tools.
```

### 3. Remix existing ideas

```
I really like how [app name, e.g., "Notion"] works, but I wish it was simpler
and focused only on [specific use case, e.g., "tracking client projects for
freelancers"]. Give me 5 ideas for a stripped-down version of this app that
solves just that one problem. Describe each in 2-3 sentences.
```

## Validating Ideas

### 4. Reality-check your idea

```
I have an idea for an app: [describe your idea in 2-3 sentences]. Can you help
me think through:

1. Who would actually pay for this?
2. What already exists that solves a similar problem?
3. What's the #1 reason this might fail?
4. What's the smallest version I could build to test if people want it?

Be honest — I'd rather hear hard truths now than waste weeks building the wrong thing.
```

### 5. Talk to potential users (prep)

```
I want to validate my app idea: [describe your idea]. Write me 5 short,
conversational questions I can ask potential users (friends, social media
followers, online communities) to find out if they'd actually use this. The
questions should NOT lead them toward saying yes — I want honest feedback.
```

## Defining Target Users

### 6. Create a user persona

```
My app idea is [describe your idea]. Help me create a simple user persona —
a fictional character who represents my ideal user. Include:

- Name and age
- Job or daily life
- Their biggest frustration related to [your app's problem area]
- What they've tried before to solve it
- What would make them say "I NEED this app"

Write it like a short story, not a boring template.
```

### 7. Find your niche

```
I want to build [describe your idea]. Help me narrow down my target audience
from "everyone" to a specific, reachable group of people. Give me 5 possible
niche audiences, and for each one explain:

- Why this group needs it most
- Where I can find them online
- How big this group might be
```

## Competitive Analysis

### 8. Understand the landscape

```
I'm thinking about building [describe your app idea]. Can you research what
already exists in this space? For each competitor, tell me:

- Name and website
- What they do well
- What users complain about (based on common review themes)
- Their pricing model
- The gap or opportunity I could fill

List at least 5 competitors, ranging from big players to small indie tools.
```

## Naming Your App

### 9. Brainstorm app names

```
I need a name for my app. Here's what it does: [describe your app in 1-2
sentences]. My target users are [describe audience]. The vibe I want is
[pick some: friendly / professional / playful / minimal / bold / techy / warm].

Give me 20 name ideas. Mix short made-up words, descriptive names, and
clever combos. For each name, note if the .com domain is likely available
(short/unique names usually are, common words usually aren't).
```

## Defining MVP Scope

### 10. Scope your MVP (Minimum Viable Product)

```
My app idea is: [describe your full vision in a paragraph].

Help me ruthlessly cut this down to an MVP — the absolute smallest version
I can build and put in front of real users. I want to launch in [timeframe,
e.g., "2 weeks"].

Give me:
- The 3-5 core features that MUST be in v1
- Features that can wait for v2
- Features that sound cool but might never be needed
- A one-sentence description of the MVP
```

### 11. Write a one-page plan

```
I'm a solo non-technical founder using AI tools to build my first app.
My idea is: [describe idea].

Write me a simple, one-page project plan that includes:
- Problem I'm solving (1-2 sentences)
- Who it's for (1 sentence)
- Core features for v1 (bullet list, max 5)
- Tech I'll use (suggest simple, beginner-friendly options)
- How I'll get my first 10 users
- What success looks like after 30 days

Keep it short enough to fit on a single page. No jargon.
```

### 12. Prioritize features with the "Would they pay for it?" test

```
Here are the features I'm considering for my app:

- [Feature 1]
- [Feature 2]
- [Feature 3]
- [Feature 4]
- [Feature 5]
- [Feature 6]

For each feature, rate it on two scales (1-5):
1. How much would users WANT this?
2. How hard is it to build?

Then sort them into: "Build first," "Build later," and "Maybe never." Explain
your reasoning in plain English.
```

## 💡 Tips for Getting Better Results

- **Be specific.** "An app for dog walkers to schedule walks" beats "a scheduling app."
- **Share context.** Tell the AI about your experience level, timeline, and budget.
- **Iterate.** If the first answer isn't great, say "These are too complicated. Give me simpler ideas aimed at [specific audience]."
- **Push back.** Say "I don't like any of these — try a completely different angle" and the AI will surprise you.
